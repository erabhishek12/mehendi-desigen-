The Ancient Art of Mehendi

Explore the rich history and cultural significance of henna, from its origins to its role in modern Indian weddings.

Read More

Mehendi, or henna, is an ancient art of temporary body adornment dating back thousands of years, originating in regions like the Middle East, North Africa, and South Asia, with evidence of its use on ancient mummies. Derived from the crushed leaves of the henna plant, it has natural cooling properties and is applied as a paste to create intricate designs for cultural, religious, and festive occasions, especially for brides and grooms before weddings. Origins and History Ancient Roots: Mehendi has been practiced for at least 5,000 years, with some scholars suggesting origins over 9,000 years ago. Cooling Properties: The art form started with people in desert regions using henna paste to soak their palms and feet for a cooling sensation. Cultural Spread: The practice migrated and was adopted across various cultures, with evidence of its use found on mummies in ancient Egypt.
